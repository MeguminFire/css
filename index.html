Inside Your Computer

What is a Computer?

A computer is an amazing electronic device designed to follow instructions and process information—fast, reliably, and hundreds of times per second.

It receives input (such as typing or clicks), processes this data using its parts and program instructions, and then produces output (like what you see on the screen).

Simple Example:
Think of a computer like a super-efficient chef. You give the chef a recipe (the steps), ingredients (your files), and tools (hardware), and the chef follows everything to the letter in record time.

Types of Computers

  • Desktop
    Powerful and used at desks. Example: Your home or school desktop.
  • Laptop/Notebook
    Portable, with a folding design. Example: MacBook, Dell XPS, Lenovo ThinkPad.
  • Tablet
    Touchscreen, thin, very portable. Example: iPad or Samsung Galaxy Tab.
  • Smartphone
    A tiny computer in your pocket. Example: iPhone, Android, etc.
  • Server
    Very powerful and works for many computers at once. Example: Web servers, data centers.

Common Computer Problems & Fixes

  • Computer won’t turn on
    • Ensure cables are plugged in and switched on.
    • Try another outlet or socket.
    • Hold the power button for 5 seconds.
  • Frozen or slow computer
    • Restart the computer.
    • Close unused programs and browser tabs.
    • Scan for malware or viruses using an antivirus tool.
  • No internet connection
    • Check Wi-Fi is turned on and you’re connected.
    • Restart your modem or router.
    • Test Wi-Fi with another device (is it the PC or the whole house?).
  • Black screen but computer is on
    • Try waking up your computer (press keys or move mouse).
    • Check if monitor cables are secure.
    • Try another monitor or device.
  • Computer is noisy or overheats
    • Make sure fans aren’t blocked by dust—clean as needed.
    • Don’t place the computer right against a wall; let it “breathe”.
    • If it’s still loud, ask a tech expert—it could be a failing fan or drive.
Tip: If you’re unsure, write down what happened and ask a friend, family member, or tech support. Never be afraid to ask!
